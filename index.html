COVID-19 Endemic in Malaysia

The COVID-19 pandemic struck Malaysia in the middle of 2020 and caused a significant impact on the lives of people and the economy. This visualization aims to show the trend and distribution of crucial data points from multiple perspectives in Malaysia during the Endemic phase in 2022.

Daily COVID-19 Cases Reported

Without a doubt, Malaysia had been going in the right direction as evidenced by the downward slope in the number of cases reported post-endemic. The increase in cases during March, with a peak of 33,406 cases on March 5th, was due to the viral Omicron variant circulating in the country. Additionally, the slight increase in cases during July, with a peak of 5685 cases on July 20th, was due to the increased travel activity.

Number of Death Reported by Months

After April, the death count had remain at a consistently low level as third dose vaccination rate increases past 70% of the population.

From the waterfall chart, we can see that the number of death has a strong correlation with the number of daily cases reported above. The number of death reached its peak during March with 2110 deaths reported then decreased drastically after the Omicron variant subsides.

Economical Stress Level

Many industries and economies in Malaysia were affected by COVID-19 and are struggling to recover. Click on the legend to filter for each stress level.

Kedah, Kelantan and Perak are affected the highest as they are highly dependent on labour services in the farming industry which was impacted heavily.

Since the release of the vaccines, Malaysians had been very supportive of getting vaccinated. Among the vaccines provided, Pfizer from the US appears to be the most preferred with 62% distribution rate. This is then followed by Sinovac from China with 30% distribution rate.

Case Fatality Rate (CFR) by Age Group & Vax Status

It appears that the Case Fatality Rate is the highest among all age groups for Unvaccinated individuals, showing that vaccines are effective in reducing the risk of death. Fully Vaccinated or Boosted people are generally safer except for older aged people where the risk is higher.

Population Death by Age Group & Gender

Most Common Age Group

Most death from COVID-19 comes from people of old age which are above 60 years old. This could be due to older people having more health complications hence vaccines may not be applicable.

Gender Affects Death Risk

On average, females have a lower number of deaths in each age group. Clinical research shows that females have a lower risk of dying after hospitalization compared to males.
